# Pulling

-- Pulling Only When Braking Checklist --
1. Does the pulling only happen when you press the brake pedal, or also when cruising?
2. Does it pull harder to one side the harder you press the brakes?
3. Have you had any brake work done recently, like new pads, rotors, or a caliper job?
4. After driving for a while, does one wheel feel hotter than the others when you stand near it?
5. Do you smell anything burning or notice any smoke after a longer drive?
6. Does the steering wheel jerk in your hands the moment you start braking, or does the pull build up gradually?
7. Does it pull the same direction every single time you brake, or does the direction vary?

-- Steady Drift While Cruising Checklist --
1. Does the car drift the same direction the entire time you're driving straight on the highway?
2. When was the last time you had the wheels aligned or had any steering or suspension work done?
3. Have you bumped a curb, hit a deep pothole, or had any accident even a small one in the last few months?
4. Do you have to hold the steering wheel slightly off-center to make the car go straight?
5. Have you checked the air pressure in all four tires recently, and are they all roughly the same?
6. Have any of the tires been replaced or rotated in the last few weeks?
7. Does the car drift even when you let go of the wheel briefly on a flat empty parking lot?

-- Pulling Only During Acceleration Checklist --
1. Does the car only pull when you step on the gas hard, like merging onto the highway or passing?
2. Does the steering wheel tug or twist in your hands when you accelerate firmly?
3. Does the car straighten back out as soon as you ease off the gas?
4. Does it pull the opposite direction when you let off the gas or slow down?
5. Is this a front-wheel-drive car, and has it always done this since you bought it, or did it start recently?
6. Have you had any work done on the engine mounts, axles, or CV joints recently?
7. Does it pull more when the road is wet or when one wheel is on a different surface than the other?

-- Drift That Follows the Road's Slope Checklist --
1. When you drive on a perfectly flat parking lot with no slope, does the car still pull or does it go straight?
2. Does the pull only show up on certain roads or in certain lanes?
3. Does the direction of the pull change depending on which lane you're in or which road you're on?
4. Do you find that you're constantly making small steering corrections to stay in your lane?
5. Has anyone else driven the car and noticed the same drift, or is it something only you feel?
6. Did the drift start suddenly or has it always been there since you got the car?
7. When you cross a bridge or get on a road that's tilted the other direction, does the pull reverse?

-- Pull That Started After Recent Tire or Service Work Checklist --
1. Did the pulling start right after a tire rotation, new tire installation, or wheel alignment?
2. About how many miles or days passed between the service and the start of the pulling?
3. Did the shop replace one tire by itself, or were they all replaced together?
4. Does the pull get more noticeable the faster you drive, especially over 45 or 50 miles per hour?
5. Was the car pulling before the service, just in a different direction or to a different degree?
6. Did the shop mention any other concerns or recommend follow-up work at the same visit?
7. Have you taken the car back to the shop to have them re-check, and what did they say?

-- Wandering or Drifting in Both Directions Checklist --
1. Does the car wander back and forth on its own, instead of pulling steady to one side?
2. Does the steering feel loose, like there's slack or play before the wheels respond?
3. Do you hear any clunking, knocking, or popping noises from the front end when you go over bumps?
4. Does the car feel worse and harder to control when the road is rough or uneven?
5. Have you noticed any tires wearing unevenly, especially on the inside or outside edges?
6. Does the steering wheel sit straight when the car is going straight, or is it tilted off-center?
7. Does the wandering get worse at highway speeds or stay about the same at all speeds?
