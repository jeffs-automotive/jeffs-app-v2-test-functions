# Smoke

-- White Smoke From Tailpipe Checklist --
1. Does the smoke only appear for the first minute or two after starting up cold, then disappear once the engine warms up?
2. Does it keep happening even after you've been driving for ten or fifteen minutes?
3. Does the smoke have a sweet or syrupy smell to it?
4. Have you had to add coolant or top off the radiator recently, or noticed the coolant level dropping?
5. Has the engine been running hotter than normal or has the temperature gauge crept up?
6. Is the smoke thin and wispy, or thick and heavy like a cloud?
7. Have you noticed any milky or frothy stuff on the underside of the oil filler cap?

-- Blue or Gray Smoke From Tailpipe Checklist --
1. Does the smoke puff out mainly when you first start the car after it's been sitting overnight?
2. Does it show up when you press the gas hard, like accelerating onto a highway?
3. Does it appear when you're slowing down or coasting down a hill with your foot off the gas?
4. Have you been adding oil between oil changes, and if so, how often?
5. Does the smoke smell more like burning oil than anything sweet or like raw fuel?
6. Is there a turbocharger on the vehicle that you know of, and has it been making any whining or whistling noises?
7. Have you noticed any oily film or buildup around the tailpipe tip when you wipe a finger in it?

-- Black Smoke From Tailpipe Checklist --
1. Does the black smoke puff out when you stomp on the gas, or is it there all the time?
2. Is the vehicle a diesel, or does it run on regular gasoline?
3. Have you noticed the fuel mileage dropping recently?
4. Does the engine seem to hesitate, surge, or run rough?
5. When was the last time the air filter was changed, if you remember?
6. Is the check engine light on, and does it stay on or flash?
7. Do you smell strong raw fuel along with the smoke, almost like gasoline or diesel fumes?

-- Smoke From Under the Hood Checklist --
1. Does the smoke have a sweet smell, a burnt-oil smell, or more of a plastic or electrical burn smell?
2. Did the temperature gauge climb into the red or did a hot-engine warning come on before you saw smoke?
3. Have you noticed any puddles, drips, or wet spots under the car after parking?
4. Does the smoke seem to be coming from one specific spot, or is it billowing out from all around the engine?
5. Did the smoke start right after a recent oil change or other work done on the vehicle?
6. Is the smoke only showing up after you've been driving for a while, or does it appear right away on startup?
7. Did you hear any popping, hissing, or boiling sounds along with the smoke?

-- Smoke or Burning Smell From a Wheel Checklist --
1. Is the smoke coming from one specific wheel, or do all four wheels look hot?
2. Does the vehicle pull to one side when you're driving straight on a flat road?
3. After a drive, does one wheel feel much hotter than the others when you hold a hand near it (without touching)?
4. Did you maybe leave the parking brake on, even partly, during your last drive?
5. Have the brakes felt soft, grabby, or like they're dragging when you let off the pedal?
6. Does the smoke smell more like hot metal and burning brake material, or more like burning rubber from a tire?
7. Did you just come off a long downhill stretch or a lot of stop-and-go traffic before noticing the smoke?

-- Smoke or Strong Smell Inside the Cabin Checklist --
1. Does the smoke or smell only come out when the heater or air conditioner is running?
2. Does it smell more like burning plastic and electrical, or more like burning leaves and dust?
3. Did this start the first time you turned on the heat for the season after a long stretch of not using it?
4. Are any dashboard warning lights on, or have any electrical features like windows, fans, or lights been acting up?
5. Is the smoke visible coming out of the vents, or is it just a smell with no visible smoke?
6. Does the smell get stronger when you turn the fan speed up?
7. Have you been able to pull over and stop the car safely, or is this happening while you're calling from the road?
