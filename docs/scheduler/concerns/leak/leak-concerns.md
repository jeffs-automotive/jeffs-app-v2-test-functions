# Leak

-- Brown or Black Puddle (Engine Oil) Checklist --
1. Is the puddle showing up under the front or middle of the car, roughly under the engine?
2. Does the spot feel thick and slippery between your fingers, almost like cooking oil?
3. Have you smelled anything burning or seen smoke coming from under the hood while driving?
4. Has the oil-can warning light on your dashboard turned on at all?
5. Have you had to add engine oil between oil changes lately, or is the dipstick reading low?
6. About how big is the spot — a few drops the size of a quarter, a saucer-sized stain, or a wider puddle?
7. Does it only leak after you've been driving, or do you see fresh drops even after the car has sat overnight?

-- Green, Orange, Yellow, or Pink Puddle (Antifreeze / Coolant) Checklist --
1. Does the fluid look bright or neon-colored — green, orange, yellow, or pink?
2. Have you noticed a sweet smell, kind of like maple syrup or pancake syrup, around the car or inside the cabin?
3. Has the temperature gauge been creeping toward hot, or has the car overheated recently?
4. Is the puddle showing up under the front of the car, near the radiator area?
5. Have you had to add antifreeze to the reservoir under the hood, or has the level dropped?
6. Do you see any steam rising from under the hood when you stop after a drive?
7. Does the inside of the windshield fog up oddly, or do you smell that sweet smell from the vents when the heater is on?

-- Red or Pink Puddle (Transmission or Power Steering Fluid) Checklist --
1. Where is the leak showing up — more toward the middle of the car or up near the front by the engine?
2. When you turn the steering wheel, does it feel heavier than usual or make a whining or groaning noise?
3. When you shift into Drive or Reverse, does it hesitate, slip, or feel rough?
4. Is the fluid bright red or pink, or has it darkened to a brownish-red color?
5. Have you had to top off the power steering reservoir under the hood recently?
6. Does the leak happen more when the car is parked after driving, or also when it sits unused for a day?
7. Do you mainly see the spot after the car has been running, or is it there first thing in the morning too?

-- Clear, Yellow, or Light Brown Puddle (Brake Fluid — Safety Concern) Checklist --
1. Does the brake pedal feel soft, spongy, or sink lower than normal when you press it?
2. Has the brake pedal ever gone almost all the way to the floor?
3. Has the red brake warning light or the ABS light come on recently?
4. Is the fluid slick and oily but clear-to-yellowish, and does it have an unpleasant fishy or oily smell?
5. Where is the spot showing up — near a wheel, under the middle of the car, or up under the engine bay on the driver's side?
6. Does the car pull to one side when you brake, or does stopping take longer than it used to?
7. Have you checked the small reservoir under the hood marked "brake fluid" — does it look low?

-- Clear, Odorless Puddle (Likely Water / AC Condensation) Checklist --
1. Does the puddle show up only after you've been running the air conditioner, especially on a warm or humid day?
2. Is the fluid completely clear, with no color and no smell at all?
3. Is the spot small — like a few drops or a wet patch — and toward the front-passenger side of the car?
4. Does the spot dry up quickly and leave no stain or residue behind?
5. Have you noticed any wet carpet inside the car, especially on the passenger-side floorboard?
6. Does the leak ever appear when the AC has not been on, or only after AC use?
7. Have you driven through any deep puddles or had heavy rain recently that could have left water behind?

-- Thick Dark Brown Puddle With Strong Smell (Gear / Differential Oil) Checklist --
1. Is the spot showing up under the very back of the car, near the rear axle, or under a four-wheel-drive vehicle's middle area?
2. Does the fluid smell strong and unpleasant — kind of like rotten eggs or sulfur?
3. Is the fluid thick and dark — darker and heavier-looking than regular engine oil?
4. Do you hear any whining, humming, or grinding noise from the back of the car that gets louder with speed?
5. Have you felt any vibrations or clunking, especially during turns or when accelerating?
6. Does the leak look like it's coming from a round, pumpkin-shaped housing on the rear axle?
7. Has the vehicle been used recently for towing, off-roading, or hauling heavy loads?

-- Blue or Light Blue Puddle (Windshield Washer Fluid) Checklist --
1. Is the fluid a bright blue or blue-green color, and does it have a soapy or chemical smell?
2. Does the spot show up near the front of the car, just behind the front bumper?
3. When you press the washer button, does any fluid actually reach the windshield, or has the spray weakened?
4. Have you had to refill the washer fluid reservoir more often than usual?
5. Does the fluid feel watery and thin rather than oily?
6. Does the leak happen only after you've used the windshield washers, or does it drip all the time?
7. Have you recently been in cold weather where the washer fluid lines could have frozen and cracked?
