# Tires

-- Visible Damage (Nail / Screw / Bulge / Cut) Checklist --
1. Which tire is it — front-left, front-right, rear-left, rear-right, or are you not sure?
2. What do you see — a nail or screw sticking out, a bubble or bulge in the side, a cut or gash, or something else?
3. Is the damage on the flat part of the tire that touches the road, or on the curved side wall of the tire?
4. Is the tire holding air right now, or is it going flat?
5. Is the car drivable right now, or is it parked because the tire is too low to drive on?
6. Do you have a spare tire on the vehicle, or is the damaged tire still mounted?
7. Did this happen suddenly today, or have you been driving on it for a few days?

-- Tire Going Flat / Losing Air Checklist --
1. Which tire keeps losing air — front-left, front-right, rear-left, rear-right, or more than one?
2. Did the tire go flat suddenly, or has it been slowly losing air over days or weeks?
3. How often are you having to add air — every day, every week, or every month?
4. Did you hear a hissing sound when it happened, or did you just notice it was low?
5. Have you driven over anything sharp recently, hit a pothole, or scraped a curb?
6. Have you looked the tire over and seen anything stuck in it like a nail or screw?
7. Is the car drivable to the shop right now, or does it need to be towed?

-- Low Pressure Warning Light Only Checklist --
1. Is the warning light steady on, or is it flashing or blinking?
2. If it flashes, does it blink for about a minute and then stay solid, or does it just stay blinking?
3. Have you checked the tires and do any of them actually look low?
4. Did the light come on after a cold morning, or did it come on while driving on a warm day?
5. Have you added air recently and the light still won't turn off?
6. Have you had new tires put on or had the tires off the vehicle recently?
7. Has the light been coming on and off, or has it stayed on without going away?

-- Uneven Tire Wear / Bald Spots Checklist --
1. Where is the wear showing up — the inside edge, outside edge, center of the tread, or in patchy spots around the tire?
2. Is it happening on one tire, both front tires, both rear tires, or all four?
3. Does the tire look or feel bumpy and scalloped when you run your hand across the tread?
4. When was the last time the tires were rotated or had an alignment done?
5. Are you noticing this with any vibration in the steering wheel or seat while driving?
6. Does the vehicle pull to one side when you're driving on a flat, straight road?
7. Do you know about how many miles are on this set of tires?

-- Dry Rot / Sidewall Cracking Checklist --
1. Are you seeing small cracks in the rubber on the side of the tire, the tread, or both?
2. Are the cracks just on the surface, or do they look deep enough to put a fingernail into?
3. Do you know roughly how old the tires are, or about how many years you've had them?
4. Does the vehicle sit parked for long stretches without being driven?
5. Is it on one tire, or are you seeing the same cracking on all of them?
6. Have any of the tires lost air recently or shown a pressure warning?
7. Is the car parked outside in the sun most of the time, or kept in a garage?

-- Just Want New Tires Checklist --
1. Are you replacing all four tires, just the front pair, just the rear pair, or only one?
2. Do you know what brand or model of tire is currently on the vehicle, or do you want a recommendation?
3. Are you looking for the lowest-cost option, a mid-range tire, or a longer-lasting premium tire?
4. Do you do mostly highway driving, mostly around-town driving, or a mix of both?
5. Do you drive in snow or heavy rain regularly, or mostly dry-weather driving?
6. Has the vehicle had an alignment in the last year, or would you like us to check it with the new tires?
7. Are you planning to keep this vehicle for several more years, or only another year or two?

-- Recent Tire Work Then New Symptom Checklist --
1. What work was done — new tires, a rotation, a patch or plug, a balance, or a flat repair?
2. Roughly when was the work done — a few days ago, a week ago, or longer?
3. What is the new symptom — vibration, noise, pulling, a warning light, or the tire losing air again?
4. At what speed does the issue show up — only on the highway, only at lower speeds, or all the time?
5. If it's a vibration, do you feel it more in the steering wheel or in the seat?
6. Did the same shop that did the work get a chance to look at it again, or is this the first time it's being checked?
7. Was a tire pressure sensor disturbed, replaced, or does the warning light keep coming on since the work was done?
